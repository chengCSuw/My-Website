Early Education

      When I initially arrived in America, I was a soon-to-be 5th-grade student. At the beginning of the school year, I enrolled at Dunlap Elementary School. Later, once my English proficiency had improved, I was transferred to Martin Luther King Jr. Elementary School. After completing my elementary education, I transitioned to Aki Kurose Middle School. The ESL classes I attended there enhanced my English fluency, ultimately contributing to my improved performance in high school.

      The highlights of these few years included making a decent number of friends, performing well in school, and significantly improving my English skills. However, social anxiety made my last two years of middle school quite challenging.

      I attended Franklin High School for high school education, and those four years were quite enjoyable. I maintained decent grades and formed friendships along the way. In my sophomore year, I became a part of the University of Washington's (UW) Upward Bound program. Upward Bound proved to be an invaluable learning experience, primarily due to its UW summer academy. This academy prepared me for the college journey by offering college-level courses held at UW. Additionally, I regularly attended their office hours whenever they visited my school, seeking assistance with financial aid and the college application process, which significantly smoothened the application process for me.

In my senior year, I became a member of the National Honor Society. While I volunteered at several of the organization's events, the most significant one for me was the homeless shelter donation campaign. I played a important role in organizing and promoting this event, providing me with my first real taste of leadership. I take pride in having contributed to improving the lives of disadvantaged individuals through this campaign.

University of Washington
      I am currently a senior at the University of Washington, pursuing a degree in Computer Science with an expected graduation date in 2024. Over the past academic year, I worked as a web developer for the Sensor, Automation, and Energy Laboratory. In this role, I collaborated with a team of developers to both maintain and implement enhancements for websites associated with the Lab. My post-graduation goal is to secure a position as a software engineer or wireless engineer at a prominent company. For now, I am actively searching for internships and research opportunities to gain valuable real-world experience before I graduate.
